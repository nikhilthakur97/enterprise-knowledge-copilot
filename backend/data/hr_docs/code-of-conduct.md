# Code of Conduct

**Owner:** People Operations and Legal
**Effective date:** 2026-01-01
**Applies to:** All Lumina employees, contractors, interns, and
visitors at Lumina premises or events.

Lumina is committed to a workplace that is professional, respectful,
and inclusive. This Code of Conduct sets the minimum behavioural
standards expected of everyone at the company.

## 1. Respectful workplace

We expect every Lumina employee to treat colleagues, customers,
partners, and candidates with respect. This includes:

- Listening actively and assuming positive intent.
- Disagreeing with ideas, not people.
- Using inclusive language and accommodating differences in
  communication style, language fluency, and cultural background.
- Refusing to participate in gossip about colleagues.

## 2. Anti-harassment and anti-discrimination

Lumina prohibits harassment, bullying, and discrimination on the
basis of (but not limited to): race, ethnicity, national origin,
religion, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation,
age, disability, marital status, pregnancy, or veteran status.

Examples of prohibited conduct include:

- Slurs, jokes, or imagery that demeans a protected group.
- Unwanted physical contact or sustained unwanted attention.
- Threats, intimidation, or persistent hostile behaviour toward a
  colleague.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith.

## 3. Conflicts of interest

A conflict of interest is any situation where a personal interest
might compromise — or appear to compromise — your judgement on
behalf of Lumina. Disclose conflicts as soon as you become aware of
them.

Examples requiring disclosure:

- Outside employment or consulting that overlaps with Lumina's
  business.
- A close personal relationship with a colleague in your reporting
  chain or with a vendor you select.
- Equity, board, or advisory roles in companies that compete with
  or sell to Lumina.

Disclosures are made through the conflict-of-interest form in the
People Operations portal. Disclosure is not by itself a violation;
failing to disclose is.

## 4. Confidentiality

You will encounter information at Lumina — customer data, financial
results, product plans, security details — that must not be shared
outside the company. The Confidentiality and Information Handling
section of the IT Security policy lists data classifications and
how each must be handled.

You remain bound by confidentiality obligations after leaving the
company.

## 5. Social media and public statements

You are welcome to discuss your work at Lumina on personal
channels, subject to:

- Make clear that views are your own, not Lumina's.
- Do not disclose confidential information.
- Do not represent yourself as an authorised spokesperson without
  approval from Communications.

Customer complaints raised on social media should be forwarded to
Customer Support, not engaged with personally.

## 6. Gifts and hospitality

- Modest gifts (under $50) from vendors or partners are acceptable.
- Anything above $50 must be declined or disclosed to People
  Operations and Finance.
- You may not give gifts to government officials in any amount.

## 7. Reporting violations

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code, you
have several reporting options:

1. Talk to your manager.
2. Contact People Operations directly at
   `people-ops@lumina.example`.
3. Use the anonymous ethics line at
   `ethics@lumina.example` (monitored by an external provider).

Lumina does not tolerate retaliation against anyone who reports a
concern in good faith. Allegations of retaliation are themselves
investigated under this policy.

## 8. Investigation and consequences

People Operations, sometimes with Legal, investigates reports
promptly and confidentially. Outcomes range from coaching to
termination of employment, depending on severity, intent, and
pattern. Confirmed retaliation is treated as a severe violation.
