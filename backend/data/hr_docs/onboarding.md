# Onboarding Guide

**Owner:** People Operations
**Effective date:** 2026-01-01
**Applies to:** All new Lumina employees during their first 90 days.

This guide describes what new hires can expect during their first day,
first week, and first month at Lumina, and what their manager and
buddy are responsible for delivering.

## 1. Before day one

People Operations will email you the following at least 5 working
days before your start date:

- A welcome packet with your offer letter, NDA, and tax forms.
- A laptop shipping confirmation (or pickup instructions if local).
- Calendar invitations for your first-week orientation sessions.
- Your assigned **onboarding buddy** — a peer outside your direct
  reporting line who acts as a low-stakes contact for questions.

Please complete and return all forms at least 2 working days before
your start date so accounts can be provisioned on time.

## 2. Day one

Your first day is intentionally light. You will:

1. Pick up or unbox your equipment.
2. Sign in to your Lumina account and complete MFA enrollment (see
   the IT Security policy).
3. Attend a 60-minute welcome session with People Operations.
4. Have a 1:1 with your direct manager.
5. Have a coffee chat with your onboarding buddy.

You are not expected to write or ship code on day one.

## 3. First week

Goals for the first week:

- Complete **mandatory training** in the learning portal:
  Code of Conduct, Information Security, and Anti-Harassment. These
  are non-negotiable and must be completed before the end of week 2.
- Read your team's onboarding doc and the engineering handbook.
- Pair with at least two teammates on existing work.
- Set up your local development environment using your team's
  documented runbook. Flag any gaps in the runbook to your buddy.
- Introduce yourself in the company-wide #introductions channel.

## 4. First 30 days

Goals:

- Ship a small, well-scoped first contribution (a "starter task")
  approved by your manager.
- Have at least one 1:1 with each of your immediate teammates.
- Attend a company all-hands.
- Schedule a 30-day check-in with your manager. Bring questions
  about role expectations, success metrics, and any blockers.

## 5. First 60 and 90 days

- 60-day check-in: review progress against the goals set at 30 days.
- 90-day check-in: probationary review. Your manager confirms
  successful completion of the probation period or, in rare cases,
  extends or ends it.

## 6. Equipment and accounts

You will be issued:

- A company laptop (MacBook Pro or equivalent), pre-imaged.
- Accounts for: identity provider (SSO), email, code repository,
  ticket tracker, design tools (if applicable), and the People
  Operations portal.
- Access to team-specific tools is granted by your manager via the
  IT access-request process.

If anything is missing on day one, contact `it-help@lumina.example`
or open a ticket through the IT portal.

## 7. The buddy program

Your buddy is **not** your manager and **not** a mentor. Their job is
to answer the small "is this normal?" questions that you might not
want to bring to your manager — Slack etiquette, where to find a
runbook, who to ask about benefits, etc.

Buddies are paired for the first 60 days and meet at least weekly.

## 8. If something goes wrong

If you do not have a manager 1:1 scheduled by end of day one, or if
mandatory training has not been assigned by end of day two, contact
People Operations directly at `people-ops@lumina.example`.
